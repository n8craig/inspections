Section 5 Solitary

ICE PBNDS defines a “Special Management Unit” as “housing unit for detainees in administrative or disciplinary segregation” and defines segregation as “[c]onfinement in an individual cell isolated from the general population; for administrative, disciplinary, or protective reasons” (ICE 2016, 473, 474). These are anodyne euphemisms for solitary confinement. The G-324A SIS form records information on four kinds of solitary confinement:

  • administrative
  • disciplinary
  • medical
  • mental health

During the 21.83 months covering the period between September 6, 2019 and July 1, 2021, there were 37998 instances of solitary reported (Tables 5.1, 5.2, and Figure 5.1).

There was an average of 184.79 (SD=357.92) instances of segregation per facility by inspection. Six facilities have significantly higher than average number of solitary instances reported per inspection. Of these facilities two are managed by ICE, one is managed by the Henderson Police Department, two are operated by CoreCivic, and one is managed by LaSalle Corrections.

Roughly half of the total instances of solitary were administrative segregation (45.53%, n = 17302) while disciplinary (25.65%, n = 9745) and medical (26.18%, n =9948) segregation made up nearly the other half. Mental health segregation (2.64%, n = 1003) comprised a relatively small proportion of the total number of solitary instances and still surpassed 1000 instances.

5.1 Solitary Over 60 Days

The G-324A SIS form includes line items for annual number of administrative and disciplinary segregation stays over 60 days. ICE PBNDS Appendix 3.1.A, Offense Categories I.B.3 defines up to 60 days as the maximum length of disciplinary segregation (ICE 2016, 224). It is unclear why form G-324A has an entry for reporting uses of disciplinary solitary exceeding the 60 day maximum defined by PBNDS.

Based on the inspections reviewed there were 400 instances of segregation > 60 days reported (Figures 5.2, 5.3, and Table 5.3). Of these cases, 87% (n=348) involved administrative solitary and 13% (n=52) involved disciplinary solitary.

Within this sample of inspections, there were on average 1.86 (SD=5.23) instances of segregation greater than 60 days per inspection. There are five facilities that have significantly higher than average number of incidents of solitary greater than 60 days. Of these, one is operated by MTC, three by the GEO Group, and one by CoreCivic. MTC’s Imperial Regional Detention Facility stands out with more than double the next two highest ranking facilities regarding solitary > 60 days.
